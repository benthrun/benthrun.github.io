Spcecraft Design Final Project

The final project for my spacecraft design course was a theoretical design for NASA's CubeSat launch intiative. This project challenged my team to work together to create a meaningful mission in respect to NASA scientists as well as how the mission would be pulled off.

The mission that was agreed upon, used Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) to measure ice elevation at the North and South Poles. The team felt this was an important mission, since climate change threatens human life and the ability to monitor the ice elevation is critical. The aspects of this project that I worked on include: Abstract, Relevance to NASA, SAR Instrument Research, Data Handling Subsystem, Electrical Power Subsystem, Thermal Subsystem, Risk Assessment.

Even with the coronavirus disrupting this project, the team I was a part of was able to succefully produce a project which met all of the requirements posed by both the instructor as well as the NASA's CubeSat launch intiative.
